Hi, my name is Juhyun Do

Introduction

Life is truly full of surprises, and I’m embracing every twist and turn! Hi, my name is Juhyun Do. I’m a sophomore at UC Berkeley studying Electrical Engineering & Computer Science, driven by a passion for sustainability and cutting-edge technology. Only a year ago, I was assisting students with special needs at a local high school in Korea during my two-year mandatory conscription. Before that, as a freshman in 2021, I was diving into nuclear engineering, fueled by my fascination with carbon-free energy and fusion. Today, my focus has shifted to the realm of hardware-accelerated AI and efficient computing.

Still, my core theme and passion remain unchanged: finding innovative ways to make the world a more sustainable place and improving human lives. My curiosity has been further shaped by reading books like Chip War, The Coming Wave, and The Code Breaker, which made me realize the transformative power of AI, semiconductors, and synthetic biology that will shape the next stage of humanity. I’m excited to contribute to the responsible and ethical development of these innovations.

In my free time, you can find me reading, watching epic films like Inception and Dune, staying active with ping-pong, running, and gym workouts, or enjoying the golden hour over the sea. Let’s see where life—and my love for technology—takes me next!

My Projects (recent to old)

Self-Attention

October - December 2024

Member of a IEEE Berkeley Speech-to-Text team. Transformed audio into a time-frequency representatio after removing noise. Visualized the audio data using spectogram. Fed into self-attention algorithm, calculated attention weights, and visualized them using heat map.

Voice Recognition

November 2024

Developed a voice recognition system using Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) and Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to classify audio signals based on syllables and intonation using Python. Conducted dimensionality reduction techniques to enhance computational efficiency. Achieved accuracy of average of 97.9% for 4 words.

Ngordnet (N-Grams and WordNet)

October 2024

Efficiently parsed, queried, and analyzed large-scale word frequency data of word usage over default value of 120 years. Constructed a graph model using adjacency list to represent hypernym-hyponym relationships. Implemented BFS-based traversal and recursive helper methods to extract all hyponyms of a given word or set of words.

Shazam

September 2024

Applied Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) and spectrogram techniques to analyze audio signals and extract meaningful fingerprints using Scipy and Numpy. Implemented peak detection algorithms to identify unique audio features. Achieved accurate song identification using a fingerprint database and hashing techniques.
